Process
Result

Arduino, circutry, electronic packaging, soldering, Solidworks, 3D printing.

Trac

Winter 2014, Northwestern University

Challenge

NUvention Medical is a 20-week long entrepreneurial class that allowed students to develop new medical innovations with a interdisciplinary team of students from engineering school, law school, business school and medical school.

Process

Our team started with shadowing in operating rooms to observe how medical professionals perform their day to day tasks. After a process of needs identification, brainstorming, and field shadowing, our team developed a tool for physical therapists to better track patient's muscle recovery progress.

I served as the lead design engineer to produce proof of concept prototypes, oversee product look and feel, and drive technology visions.

Result

Designed and developed a patent pending wearable device to track real time muscle activities through ethnographic research, brainstorming, iterative prototyping, and engineering mock ups.

Relevant Skills

Medical Devices, User Research, UX Design, Solidworks, Hardware Engineering

Confidential Project

Fall 2013, Northwestern University

Challenge

Acting as design consultancy for P&G, our teams were tasked to provide innovative product proposals to enhance consumer experience through human-centered design process.

Process

Partnered with P&G, we adopted their design research methods to design for the users. We conducted in-home-visits', observed user needs and lead interviews, synthesized findings, prototyped ideas, obtained user feedback with low fidelity prototypes, and eventually proposed a user-centric design that would bring delights to the mundane.

Result

Discovered great insights through in home visits and panel interviews, identified opportunity areas to bring delight in the mundane. Pitched final design proposal to client from P&G, communicated ideas through visuals, storyboards, personas, sketches, and quick mock-ups. Final prototype brings a moment of joy to a typical user experience.

Relevant Skills

P&G design research process, user research, storyboards, personas, prototyping

Spin-N-Glow

Fall 2013, Northwestern University

Challenge

Art and Engineering often goes hand in hand. In a 3 weeks design bootcamp before our MS. Engineering Design & Innovation program, we were challenged to create a physical installment around the theme "Kinetic Art" with $100 budget. This project was aimed to get us familiar with the prototyping resources availble in Northwestern.

Process

Inspired by pendulums and LED's, I set out to design a gear themed LED carousel. This design has gone through multiple iterations from sketches, foam core mockups, to CAD detail mockups.To machine the final deliverable, I have used laser cutter, CNC mill, lathe, drill press, bandsaw, and soldering tools to make all the custom pieces.

Results

A kinetic art installation with spinning disco balls and ever changing LED lights.

Relevant Skills

Machine shop tools, arduino circuit design

Power Cabinet

Cabinet infastructure, weight management, internal chassis designs | Sept 2012 - Aug 2013

Challenge

To design a power cabinet for a new diagnostic imaging system.

Process

Leveraged CAD tool (Creo 2) to layout individual component, allocated height constraints for each internal componet, arranged chassis placement to optimize for serviceability, manufacturability, and thermal performance. Designed individual chassis and worked with multiple national suppliers to obtain cabinet builds for verification activities.

Results

A new production ready power cabinet design was created after 9 months of design and development, benchmarking from existing cabinet products, and multiple design reviews facilitated to ensure robust design for reliability, manufacturability, and serviceability. New cabinet contains power supply chassis, fiber optics communication, and data processing components.

Relevant Skills

Creo 2 (Pro/Engineer), Electro-mechanical packaging, sheetmetal

Rack Mount Sheet Metal Chassis

Sept 2012 - Aug 2013

Challenge

To design rack mount sheet metal chassis for power delivery and data acquistion systems.

Process

Designed sheetmetal chassis in CAD, performed quick thermal calculations for fan selection. Strategically placed fasteners to allow for accessibility from top and sides, incorporated PEM hardware and optimized symmetry to enhance product manufacturability. Designed internal cable harnesses and created drawings.

Results

Quickly learned about sheetmetal design and electronic packaging fundamentals, delivered multiple production ready chassis with internal cables in rigorous schedule.

Relevant Skills

Creo 2 (Pro/Engineer), Electro-mechanical packaging, sheetmetal, electromechanical sheetmetal packaging design, internal harnesses

Fluid Cooling Manfold

Feb 2012 - Aug 2012 | Milwaukee WI

Challenge

To design a fluid cooling subsystem for gamma ray detectors.

Process

Identified design constraints, benchmarked existing technologies, proposed design concept using CAD, calculated pressure loss and heat transfer performance to support design decisions, leveraged supplier expertise, faciliated design reviews to drive product directions, and procured engineering prototype for testing. Design currently implemented in product release.

Results

Delivered near production fluid cooling subsystem design for solid state gamma ray detector modules, led technical design reviews to ensure product reliabilty, manufacturabilty, and serviceability.

Relevant Skills

Creo 2 (Pro/Engineer), thermal system design
